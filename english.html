Words can be Functional (grammatical) words like articles (a, an, or the), conjunctions (because, while, and, but, or so), prepositions (in, to, of, etc.), and other words whose function is primarily to express relationships among other words. Content Words: noun, pronoun, adjective, verb and adverb whose function is mainly to convey meanings. We express our thoughts or feeling using words in groups.

Sentence is a group of words that has complete thought. It begins with a capital letter and ends with a period, a question mark, or an exclamation point. Here are examples. 1. He has opened the door. 2. Has he opened the door? 3. How noisily he has opened the door! 4. Open the door. 5. Stop!

1. 2 Parts of a sentence

A sentence must: identify what or whom you’re talking about say something about that person, thing, or abstract concept. e.g. Almaz ran to the door. Here, we’re talking about(Almaz ) and a statement about her (what she did). These two parts are, known as the subject and the predicate respectively.

The subject is the who or the what that a sentence is referring to. It might be one or more words. Note examples of subjects in boldface: S1. He got a sweater for Christmas. S2. The sweater he got for Christmas was too small for him. S3.The idea that he would get yet another sweater next Christmas frustrated him S4. Joseph and John got a sweater for Christmas.

< The predicate is the part of the sentence that contains the verb and every other word in the sentence that’s not the subject. It might be only one word (the verb) or it might be a several group of words. E.g.1. I will go to Austria as soon as the rates get better. Brainstorming questions What is simple sentence? How many clauses do it contain? Simple Sentence is a kind of sentence which has at least a subject and a verb (one main clause(MC)). A sentence can get quite long and complicated and still remain a simple sentence. Note that, however, there is no dependent clause and no more than one independent (main clause) clause in a simple sentence.

The loin came. (a MC with single subject and single verb) Tiger and lion are cat families. (a MC with compound subject) Teachers read and teach. (a MC with compound verb) My father and mother said goodbye and left on the vacation. (a MC with compound verb and compound subject) He opened the letter and the box. (a MC with compound direct object) It can rain from the east or from the west. (Compound prepositional phrase) Mary writes me letters (a MC with compound object: the direct object letters and the indirect object me)
